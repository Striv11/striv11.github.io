Terabytes of data on behavioural and finance indicators of spend. How does one estimate where to spend money on, how does one predict before hand what their spend is going to be and how can organizations leverage this data. While working with American express on millions of customer I worked as the complete project owner to be reported directly to the leadership below the CEO. The task, super simple, to predict spend Time spent was over 2 years working on it when I came in to provide fresh perspective. The novel way I thought of dealing with the issue was recursively try to partition spenders on their spending behaviour by building models along the way when predictions on different other behavioural parameters were being made. Being proprietory the project cannot be displayed up here, but the essence of the process remains this; divide customers on behavioural spend parameters to smaller and smaller subsets until you come to a very similar dataset that has been previously seen (similarity found by clustering), once the dataset is found, the work is to then develop a prediction based on a model built on the training dataset which was similar to the cluster. The problem hence was solved and if you hold a credit card right now, it is based on the work the team did. The time it took? A daily grind of more than 10 hours, daily for 6 months. The effort it took, more than 500 iterations on trying to solve the problem using different strategies or by tweaking the gradient boosting algorithms developed by Tianqi Chen. The solution was creatively found in a flash of light! As is with al good things, it worked and generates over million in pure profits every year to the firm, no credit card is sold without having our footprints on it.
